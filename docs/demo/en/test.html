SINFONIA

FOR THE JOY OF COMMUNAL MUSIC

Sinfonia is Princetons orchestra:
a medley of students, faculty, and staff united by a common love of ensemble music. 

For some members, Sinfonia is an opportunity to play new, exciting, nontraditional orchestral music.

For busy students, it is a relaxed musical outlet.

For all players, Sinfonia makes space for musicians to improve on their own terms, surrounded by a friendly, supportive community.

Expanding every year, Sinfonia currently averages 80 members and performs two concerts each semester in Richardson Auditorium. Additional, more informal performances are arranged in many of Princetons unique spaces, including the Rockefeller College Common Room and the Forum at the Lewis Arts Complex.

Sinfonia is purposefully designed to complement the Princeton community. Conductor Ruth Ochs is thoughtful and deliberate in selecting fruitful, diverse compositions that are assembled with a manageable time commitment.

Above all, Sinfonia pursues the highest level of musicianship while emphasizing the joy of performing wonderful music with others.

RECENT EVENTS

Sinfonia Spring Concert

Friday, May 5, 2023
7:30 - 9:30pm

Sinfonia concludes the season with two musical reflections of Irish culture and a fun nod to Cinco de Mayo. As the first symphony by a woman performed by a major symphony orchestra in the U.S., Amy Beachs Symphony in E minor opened the door for countless women composers. She boldly incorporated Gaelic melodies and quotations from her own music into the symphony. Princeton sophomore Toussaint Santicola Jones also turned to Irish myth as the inspiration for his new piece, Naked, Upon the Road to Tara. Leonora Carringtons The Red Horses of Sidhe, a work in Princeton University Art Museums collection, served as his starting point. Sinfonia will also present the Tango and Guaracha from Morton Goulds jazz-tinged Latin-American Symphonette.
